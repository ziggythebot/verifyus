## Previous Marketing Psychology Insights

From earlier analysis, we identified:

### Pain Point Validation
- **Current positioning:** "$50K+ per fraudulent hire" (future loss, abstract)
- **Problem:** Too distant, hard to visualize, triggers Hyperbolic Discounting
- **Better positioning:** "Stop wasting 10+ hours per role on fake applicants" (present pain, concrete)

### Psychological Frameworks Applied
1. **Present Bias** - Pain NOW > future loss
2. **Hyperbolic Discounting** - We discount future costs heavily
3. **Loss Aversion** - Framing around what you're losing RIGHT NOW (time, sanity)
4. **Jobs to Be Done** - Recruiters hire this to save time, not prevent future fraud
5. **Availability Heuristic** - Recent bot flood more salient than abstract $50K figure

### Recommended Hero Copy Changes
**Current:**
"Fraudulent hires cost $50K+. Deploy verification to filter pipelines."

**Better:**
"Your recruiting team is drowning in fake applications. VerifyUS filters out bot spam before they waste 10+ hours per role."

### Pricing Psychology
- **Decoy Effect** - L3 tier makes L2 look like the smart choice
- **Anchoring** - Show manual screening cost ($2,400) first, then VerifyUS cost ($600)
- **Mental Accounting** - Frame as "time saved" not just "money saved"

### Reciprocity Strategy
Offer free bot audit: "Send us your last 100 applicants, we'll show you how many were bots (free, no signup)"
